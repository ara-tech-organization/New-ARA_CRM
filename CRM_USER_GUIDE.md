CRM PRO USER GUIDE


WHAT IS THIS CRM

1. One place to manage every client, all their leads, the telecaller workflow, and the billing.
2. Two separate logins. Agency side at slash login for your team. Client portal at slash client login for the client and their telecallers.
3. Both sides share one database. A change in one place shows up instantly in the other.



PART ONE. THE AGENCY SIDE


HOW TO LOG IN

1. Open the app. The login page appears.
2. Type your email and password.
3. Click Sign In. You land on the Dashboard.
4. Forgot your password? Ask a superadmin to reset it in Access Management.


THE LEFT SIDEBAR

1. Dashboard
2. Leads Check
3. Clients
4. Total Leads
5. Ads Comparison
6. Analytics
7. Settings
8. Client Portal
9. Access Management

Collapse and expand with the small arrow at the top.



DASHBOARD

1. Five summary cards at the top. Total Clients, Active Clients, Total Leads today, Total Spend today, Average CPL.
2. One Client Wise Performance card per client. Each card shows a META row with Form, WhatsApp, Spent, CPL, and a GOOGLE row with CTR, Avg CPC, Clicks, Impressions.
3. Top Performing Clients table at the bottom, ranked by today's leads.
4. Pick a different day with the date picker in the top right.
5. Type into the Search box to filter cards by name.
6. Click any card to drill into that client's detail page.



LEADS CHECK

1. Day by day, client by client lead totals.
2. Pick a date range. Pick one client or leave it as All Clients.
3. Table shows Date, Meta Form, Meta WhatsApp, Meta Total, Meta Fund, Total Leads, Total Spend, plus a Totals row at the bottom.
4. Click Export to PDF to share with a client.



CLIENTS

1. Master list of every client.
2. Three boxes at the top count Total, Active, Inactive.
3. Search bar finds a client by name, place, industry, or account ID.
4. Five action icons on every row. Eye for view. Pencil for edit. G for Google setup. f for Meta setup. Trash for delete.


ADD A NEW CLIENT

1. Click Add Client in the top right.
2. Type the Client Name. Mandatory.
3. Fill in Place, Industry, Address, GST Number, Onboarded date. Optional Team, SMM, SME.
4. Click Save.


CONNECT GOOGLE ADS

1. Click the G icon on the client's row.
2. Type the ten digit Google Customer ID.
3. Optionally type the Account Name.
4. Click Connect and Launch.
5. The G icon turns green when the link is active.
6. To disconnect, click the green G and choose Disconnect.


CONNECT META. TWO STEPS.

1. Click the f icon on the client's row.
2. Step one. Type the Meta Ad Account ID. Starts with act underscore. Click Save and Continue.
3. Step two. Pick the Facebook Page that runs ads for this client.
4. Click Attach and Launch.
5. Meta starts pulling ninety days of campaigns, ads, insights, and leads in the background.



CLIENT AD DETAILS

1. The deep dive page for one client.
2. Reach it by clicking a client card on the Dashboard or any row on the Clients page.
3. Top of the page has the client name and chips, a Refresh button, a Resync from Meta button when on the Meta tab, and a date filter with quick buttons for Today, 7, 14, and 30 Days.
4. Two tabs. Google Ads and Meta Ads.


GOOGLE ADS TAB. FROM TOP TO BOTTOM.

1. Banner if not linked, with a button to link from the Clients page.
2. Account Info panel.
3. Billing card. Total Added, Total Spent, Available Balance, Utilization.
4. Performance Summary. Six KPI cards.
5. Campaigns table. Click a row to expand keywords.
6. Keywords table with Match Type, Status, Quality Score, Ad Relevance, Landing Page Experience.
7. Campaign Performance chart. Daily spend trend.


META ADS TAB. FROM TOP TO BOTTOM.

1. Banner if not linked, with a button to link from the Clients page.
2. Account Info. Page Name, Ad Account, Currency and Time Zone, Date Range, Lifetime Entities chips.
3. Billing card. Ad Account Balance and Lifetime Spent. If Meta cannot be reached, you see a yellow notice instead. Ask the account manager to refresh the Meta token.
4. Performance Summary. Two rows of KPI cards. Spend, Total Leads, Form Leads, WhatsApp Leads, CPL, Reach, Impressions, Clicks, CTR, CPC, CPM.
5. Campaigns table with Daily Budget, Spend, Impressions, Clicks, CTR, Leads, CPL, Messages.
6. Campaign Performance chart.
7. Lead Forms table.
8. Leads table. The main daily workspace. Detailed next.



THE LEADS TABLE


COLUMN GROUPS

1. Lead Details. Row number, Date, Source chip, Name, Contact, Form Name, Form Responses, Email, Location, Hair or Skin, Telecaller.
2. Initial Call Details. First Call Date, Call Label, Response, Remarks.
3. Reminder. Next Follow up date.
4. Appointment. Status, Appointment Date, Booked On.
5. Follow up Status. FU number and a Latest and History list.
6. Action. Save button on every row, Trash icon only on manually entered WhatsApp leads.


FILTER BAR ABOVE THE TABLE

1. Call Label dropdown. CONNECTED, NOT CONNECTED, DISCONNECTED, RNR, BUSY, INVALID. Pick more than one.
2. Response dropdown. TREATMENT BOOKED, CONSULTED, WARM, HOT, COLD, NOT INTERESTED, NOT REQUIRED, NOT ENQUIRED, CTC, WILL CALL, DUPLICATE, CLOSED.
3. Hair or Skin dropdown.
4. Appointment dropdown.
5. Search box. Matches name, email, phone, location, remarks, telecaller, and every form answer.
6. Counter on the right shows Showing X of Y.
7. Clear all button appears whenever any filter is active.
8. Same dropdown values combine as OR. Different dropdowns combine as AND.


EDIT A ROW INLINE

1. Click any cell. The value changes instantly.
2. Click the blue Save button on the right of the row.
3. The button briefly shows Saving, then Saved in green.
4. Row colour reflects Response. Green is TREATMENT BOOKED. Sky blue is CONSULTED. Purple is DUPLICATE.


ADD A FOLLOW UP

1. In the Latest and History column, click the small plus icon at the bottom of the cell.
2. A mini row appears with FU number, date picker, Call Label, Remarks.
3. Fill it in. Click the row's Save button.
4. FU number auto increments for the next attempt.
5. Remove a follow up with the trash icon on the right of the mini row.


ADD A WHATSAPP LEAD MANUALLY

1. Click the green Add WhatsApp Lead button in the top right of the filter bar.
2. A centered dialog opens with three sections.
3. Lead Details. Name and Phone are mandatory. Email, Location, Hair or Skin, Telecaller optional.
4. Initial Call Details. First Call Date, Call Label, Response, Remarks.
5. Reminder and Appointment. Next Follow up, Appointment Status, Appointment Date, Booked On.
6. Click Add Lead.
7. The new row appears at the top with a WhatsApp source chip and Form Name WhatsApp manual entry.


DELETE A MANUAL WHATSAPP LEAD

1. Click the red trash icon next to the Save button on the row.
2. A confirmation dialog opens showing the lead's name, phone, location, and category.
3. Click Delete Lead to confirm.
4. Only manual entries can be deleted. Synced Meta leads have no trash icon.


EXPORT THE LEADS

1. Click Excel above the table for an xlsx with every column, every form question, and follow up history split into Connected and Not Connected.
2. Click PDF for a landscape A3 print version.



TOTAL LEADS

1. Pivot view of leads across every client and every date for one chosen month.
2. Click a month tab to switch months.
3. Each cell shows the total lead count for that client on that date. Meta Form plus Meta WhatsApp plus Google Call plus Google Website.
4. Excel and PDF export buttons at the top.



ADS COMPARISON

1. Side by side performance of every linked client.
2. Two tabs. Google Ads and Meta Ads.
3. Both share a date range picker, a search box, and a Refresh button.
4. Google tab columns. Account, Customer ID, Budget, Fund, Available, Impressions, Clicks, CTR, CPC, Cost, Conversions, CPL.
5. Meta tab columns. Account, Ad Account ID, Budget, Spend, Available, Reach, Impressions, Frequency, Clicks, CTR, CPC, CPM, Leads, CPL.
6. Click any row to jump into that client's detail page.



ANALYTICS

1. Detailed charts and trends for one client at a time. Meta focused.
2. Pick a client and a date range at the top.
3. Four stat cards. Meta Total Leads, Total Spend, Average CPL, Total Entries.
4. Daily Lead Trends. Three line charts. Meta Form, Meta WhatsApp, Total Meta.
5. Monthly Overview. Three bar charts for the same metrics. Toggle for Last 1, 3, 6, or 12 Months.
6. Date wise performance table with a day over day Change column.



SETTINGS

1. Personal preferences only.
2. Accent colour, dark mode, density.
3. Affects only you.



CLIENT PORTAL PAGE

1. Where you control which clients are allowed to log in to the client portal.
2. Toggle Portal Enabled on or off per client.
3. Set the seed Portal Email and Portal Password for the client's first admin user.



ACCESS MANAGEMENT

1. Manages every user who can log in to the agency side.
2. Add a user with name, email, role, password.
3. Edit, deactivate, or delete users.
4. Roles. Superadmin has everything. Admin has full data access but cannot manage users. Custom roles get the permissions you tick.



PART TWO. THE CLIENT PORTAL


HOW TO LOG IN

1. Open slash client login.
2. Type your Username or Email and Password.
3. Click Sign In.


TWO ROLES

1. Admin. Sees Google Ads, Meta Ads, Meta Leads, and the Users tab.
2. Telecaller. Sees only the Meta Leads tab.


SEED ADMIN

1. The agency creates the first admin when they enable the portal for a client.
2. That admin then creates telecaller accounts.


THE LAYOUT

1. Client name and a Logout button in the top right.
2. Four tabs. Google Ads, Meta Ads, Meta Leads, Users.
3. A shared date filter at the top of the Google and Meta tabs with quick buttons for Today, 7, 14, and 30 Days.



GOOGLE ADS TAB

1. Admins only.
2. Same view as the agency side Google Ads page, only for this client.
3. Account Info, Billing, Performance Summary, Campaigns, Keywords, Campaign Performance chart.



META ADS TAB

1. Admins only.
2. Intentionally slim. Only two things.
3. Billing card. Ad Account Balance and Lifetime Spent.
4. Leads table. Same as the Meta Leads tab.



META LEADS TAB

1. Everyone sees this.
2. Identical to the agency side Leads table. Same filters, color coded rows, inline editing, follow ups, manual entry and delete, Excel and PDF exports.


A TELECALLER'S EVERYDAY FLOW

1. Open the Meta Leads tab.
2. Filter by Next Follow up today or scroll through the list.
3. Call the lead.
4. Update Call Label, Response, and Remarks.
5. If they want to come in, set Appointment Status to APPOINTMENT BOOKED, set the Appointment Date, set Booked On to today.
6. If they need a callback, set the Next Follow up date.
7. Click the row's Save button.
8. If you spoke more than once, click the plus icon in the Latest and History column to log each attempt.
9. Move on to the next lead.


LOG A WHATSAPP OR WALK IN LEAD

1. Click Add WhatsApp Lead.
2. Fill in the dialog. Name and Phone are mandatory.
3. Click Add Lead. The new row appears at the top.
4. Continue with the call, save, and follow up steps.



USERS TAB

1. Admins only.
2. Manage who can log in to the portal for this client.
3. Table shows Username, Email, Role, Status, Actions.


ADD A TELECALLER

1. Click Add User.
2. Type the Username. Lowercase letters, digits, dots, underscores, or hyphens. Three to sixty characters.
3. Email is optional.
4. Type a password of at least six characters. Eye icon lets you peek at what you typed.
5. Pick the Role. Admin or Telecaller.
6. Click Create User.
7. Share the username and password with the telecaller.


EDIT A USER

1. Click Edit on their row.
2. Change Username, Email, Role, or reset the Password.
3. Leave Password blank to keep the current one.
4. Click Save Changes.


DEACTIVATE OR DELETE

1. Deactivate temporarily blocks login. History stays.
2. Delete is permanent.
3. You cannot delete or deactivate yourself.
4. You cannot delete or deactivate the last active admin.


LOG OUT

1. Click Logout in the top right.
2. Your session is cleared. You return to the login page.



GLOSSARY

1. AD ACCOUNT. The container on Meta or Google that holds campaigns and budget for one client.
2. CAMPAIGN. The top level of an ad. Defines the objective.
3. AD SET. Inside a campaign. Defines audience and budget. Google calls this an Ad Group.
4. AD. The actual creative. Image, video, or carousel.
5. LEAD. A person who filled a form, messaged on WhatsApp, or raised their hand.
6. FORM LEAD. A Meta lead from a Lead Ad form.
7. WHATSAPP LEAD. A Meta lead from a Click to WhatsApp ad, or one you entered manually.
8. CTR. Click Through Rate. Clicks divided by impressions, as a percentage. Higher is better.
9. CPC. Cost Per Click. Total cost divided by clicks. Lower is better.
10. CPL. Cost Per Lead. Total spend divided by leads.
11. CONVERSION. A lead that turned into a paying customer.
12. TELECALLER. The person whose job is to call leads.
13. RESYNC. A button that fetches the last ninety days from Meta again.



COMMON PROBLEMS

1. Bounced to the login screen after a refresh. Session expired. Log in again. If it keeps happening, ask the admin to reactivate your account.

2. Meta Billing card says Live billing not available. The Meta token expired or lost access. Contact the account manager. Everything else on the page still works.

3. Cannot delete a Meta form lead. By design. Only manual WhatsApp leads can be deleted.

4. Connected an account but no data is showing. First sync takes a few minutes. Up to ninety days for Meta. Click Refresh after ten minutes. Still nothing means ask the agency to check sync runs.

5. Forgot the portal password. Telecallers, ask your client portal admin to reset it. Client portal admins, ask your agency account manager.

6. Duplicate lead in the Meta Leads table. Open the row, set Response to DUPLICATE, click Save. Row turns purple.

7. Dashboard numbers do not match the Ad Details page. Dashboard is today only by default. Ad Details uses the date range you picked.



WHO LOGS IN WHERE

1. Agency admin. Slash login. Role superadmin or admin. Full access to every client.
2. Agency staff with limited access. Slash login. Sees only the pages the admin granted.
3. Client portal admin. Slash client login. Sees Google, Meta, Leads, Users for their own client.
4. Client telecaller. Slash client login. Sees only the Meta Leads tab.
