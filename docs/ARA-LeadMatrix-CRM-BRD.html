5Executive Summary

ARA Lead Matrix is a purpose-built, multi-tenant CRM that runs the day-to-day operations of a digital performance-marketing agency and gives every one of its clients a live, self-service window into their own advertising results.

The agency (ARA Discoveries) manages paid advertising on Meta (Facebook & Instagram) and Google Ads on behalf of a roster of client businesses — predominantly cosmetic, dermatology and hair/skin clinics operating in India (currency ₹ INR, business day anchored to IST). Every advertising rupee generates leads, and every lead must be called, qualified, followed up, and converted into a booked appointment and a paid treatment. Lead Matrix is the single system that connects ad spend → leads → telecalling → appointments → conversions → billing into one source of truth.

The platform is delivered as two doors into one database:

  • The Agency Console — the internal command centre where agency staff onboard clients, connect ad accounts, monitor spend and balances across the whole book of business, compare client performance, and manage users and access.
  • The Client Portal — a separately-authenticated, branded surface where each client (and their telecallers) see only their own ad performance, work their leads on an Excel-style telecalling sheet, and read live end-of-day and monthly reports.

Data flows in automatically. Campaigns, ad sets, ads, daily performance insights, lead-generation forms and the leads themselves are synchronised from Meta and Google on a recurring schedule, backed by real-time Meta webhooks for instant lead delivery. Advertising spend is reconciled into an immutable, idempotent billing ledger that keeps each client's fund balance accurate to the rupee. A change made on either side of the platform is visible on the other within roughly thirty seconds.

6Business Background

ARA Discoveries operates as a performance-marketing agency. Its commercial model is straightforward: clients place advertising funds with the agency; the agency deploys those funds across Meta and Google campaigns; the campaigns generate enquiries ("leads"); and the value of the engagement is judged by how many leads convert into booked appointments and completed treatments at a controlled cost-per-lead (CPL).

The agency's clients are principally clinics in the cosmetic, dermatology and hair/skin sector. Their sales motion is telephone-led: a lead who fills a Facebook form or messages on WhatsApp is called by a telecaller, qualified, and — if interested — booked for a consultation and, ultimately, a treatment. The quality of this telecalling operation is the difference between profitable and unprofitable ad spend, which is why the platform treats telecalling as a first-class capability rather than an afterthought.

6.1 The operating reality before Lead Matrix

Historically the agency ran its operation across three disconnected surfaces:

  • Native ad dashboards (Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads) for spend and campaign metrics — one login per client, per platform, with no cross-client view.
  • Shared spreadsheets (notably the "GROHAIR — Lead Sheet" telecalling workbook) where telecallers logged every call, response, follow-up and appointment by hand.
  • Manual reconciliation of how much had been spent against how much each client had funded, tracked informally.

6.2 Why a bespoke platform

Off-the-shelf CRMs model a generic B2B sales pipeline; they do not understand Meta lead-form ingestion, click-to-WhatsApp lead attribution, ad-account balance depletion, or an Indian clinic's telecalling vocabulary (HOT / WARM / COLD / DARMANT / CONSULTED / TREATMENT BOOKED). Lead Matrix was built specifically around these realities so the agency's existing spreadsheet workflow could be preserved exactly — the telecalling sheet round-trips to and from the operator's real Excel workbook — while everything around it became automated, multi-tenant and auditable.

7Business Vision

To operate the entire agency — every client, every ad rupee, every lead and every telecall — from one live, transparent platform that both the agency and its clients trust as the single source of truth.

7.1 Vision statement

"One platform where advertising spend, lead generation, telecalling and results are connected end-to-end, visible in real time, and provable to the client — so that no lead is lost, no budget is silently overspent, and every client can see exactly what their money is buying."

7.3 Target end-state

An agency in which onboarding a new client is a guided, one-click ad-account connection; in which every telecaller works a single prioritised queue instead of a shared spreadsheet; in which account managers manage by exception (alerts) rather than by manual monitoring; and in which clients need never ask "how are my ads doing?" because the answer is always one login away.

8Current Business Process (As Implemented)

The following describes the end-to-end operational process as the platform now supports it.

8.1 Client onboarding

Create client record ▶ Connect Google Ads (10-digit Customer ID) ▶ Connect Meta (Ad Account + Facebook Page) ▶ First historical sync (up to 90 days) ▶ Enable Client Portal & seed admin login

An account manager registers the client (name, place, industry, GST, onboarding date, assigned staff), then uses one-click wizards to attach the Google Ads customer ID and the Meta ad account & page. The system immediately begins pulling historical campaigns, spend and leads in the background. The manager optionally enables the portal and issues the client's first admin credentials.

8.2 Automated data acquisition

On a recurring schedule (Google every ~15 min; Meta every ~15 min, offset by 5 min to avoid contention), the platform synchronises campaigns, ad sets, ads, daily insights, lead forms and leads. Meta leads additionally arrive in real time via signed webhooks, with a polling safety-net and a retry queue for any that fail first-pass processing. Every advertising spend figure is reconciled into the billing ledger and debited from the client's balance.

8.3 Lead handling & telecalling (the daily core)

Lead arrives (form / WhatsApp / manual) ▶ Appears in telecaller's queue ▶ Telecaller calls & logs outcome ▶ System auto-sets next reminder ▶ Book appointment / follow-up ▶ Mark Consulted / Treatment Booked

Telecallers work the leads table (agency side) or the portal telecalling sheet. For each lead they record the call label (CONNECTED, BUSY, RNA, SWITCH OFF…), the response (HOT, WARM, COLD, CONSULTED, TREATMENT BOOKED…), remarks, appointments and follow-up attempts. The platform automatically suggests the next reminder date based on the outcome, appends call history, and flags leads as DARMANT (dormant) after five consecutive unconnected attempts.

8.4 Monitoring, reporting & billing oversight

Account managers watch the agency dashboard for low balances and "spending but zero leads" conditions. Clients and managers read the live EOD (end-of-day) report — day and month target-vs-achieved, appointment pipeline, source breakdown — and the Monthly Abstract grid. Managers add client payments (which credit the ledger) and periodically reconcile the CRM balance against the platform's reported balance.

20Business Rules

Invariant rules the system enforces regardless of user action.

Rule IDRuleRationale
RULE-01Superadmin and admin roles bypass all agency permission checks.Operational necessity for platform administration.
RULE-02A portal user's client is derived from their token, never the request.Prevents cross-tenant data access (IDOR protection).
RULE-03Meta lead processing is idempotent on the lead's unique Meta ID.Guarantees no duplicate or lost leads across webhook + poll.
RULE-04A contact number is unique within a client but may repeat across clients.Same person may be a lead for multiple clinics legitimately.
RULE-05Synced Meta-form leads cannot be deleted; only manual leads can.Protects the auditable record of paid-for leads.
RULE-06A lead flips to DARMANT after 5 consecutive not-connected attempts with no connection.Standardises dormancy so effort isn't wasted on unreachable leads.
RULE-07Next-reminder date is auto-set by outcome: BUSY→+1h; RNA→other half-day; SWITCH OFF/NOT CONNECTED→tomorrow; HOT/WARM/COLD→+2/+7/+30 days; APPOINTMENT BOOKED→day before appointment; CLOSED/INVALID/DUPLICATE→cleared.Removes manual scheduling and enforces consistent cadence.
RULE-08WhatsApp leads are counted only on ad sets optimised for CONVERSATIONS.Prevents phantom WhatsApp leads from stray messages on form ads.
RULE-09Reported lead counts come from Meta insights, never from CRM records alone.Avoids under-reporting when ingestion is temporarily broken.
RULE-10Every ledger entry carries an idempotency key; duplicate keys are rolled back.Concurrent sync runs can never double-charge a client.
RULE-11Billing balances are ledger-owned and never editable via client update.Keeps the financial record defensible and tamper-resistant.
RULE-12Sync windows are floored at the client's onboarding date.Never pulls or bills pre-onboarding activity.
RULE-13Meta billing runs in dry-run mode by default (no writes until enabled).Financial safety switch during rollout/testing.
RULE-14Dropped clients are excluded from live lists but retained with full drop history.Reversible off-boarding without data loss.
RULE-15The last active portal admin cannot be deleted, demoted or deactivated.Prevents a client from being locked out of user management.
RULE-16Legacy portal tokens without a user identity default to least privilege (telecaller).Closes a prior privilege-escalation gap.
RULE-17All monetary values are INR; the business day is calculated in IST.Correct daily roll-ups for an India-based operation.
RULE-18Telecalling sheet values are validated against the exact spreadsheet vocabulary.Guarantees clean Excel round-trip and consistent reporting.

21High-Level Process Flows

21.1 Client onboarding & activation

Register client▶ Validate & connect Google Ads▶ Connect Meta ad account▶ Select Facebook page & subscribe▶ Historical backfill (≤90d)▶ Enable portal + seed admin▶ Client live

21.2 Automated data acquisition & billing

Scheduler tick▶ Per-client: pull campaigns/adsets/ads▶ Pull daily insights & forms▶ Reconcile spend delta vs snapshot▶ Write ledger debit & update balance▶ Record sync run

21.3 Real-time lead ingestion

Meta webhook (signed)▶ Verify signature, ACK 200▶ Write raw-lead audit record▶ Resolve client via form▶ Fetch full lead, map fields▶ Upsert Lead (idempotent)▶ Appear in queue

Failures are enqueued to a retry queue with exponential backoff (1m → 5m → 15m → 1h → 6h → abandoned). A poller independently re-checks each form for any leads a webhook may have missed.

21.4 Telecalling day cycle

Open today's queue▶ Call lead▶ Log call label + response▶ Auto reminder set▶ Book appointment / add follow-up▶ Mark Consulted / Treatment Booked▶ Live reports update (~30s)

21.5 Payment & reconciliation

Client transfers funds▶ Admin records payment▶ Ledger credit + balance up▶ Ongoing spend debits▶ Periodic reconcile vs platform▶ Adjustment entry if gap

23Feature Descriptions

Narrative descriptions of every module (cross-referenced to Section 18).

M01 · Authentication & Session

Two independent, secure login experiences share one design system. Agency staff sign in with email/password; clients and telecallers sign in with username or email. Sessions are stateless tokens carried in a secure cookie or header; passwords are bcrypt-hashed. Login endpoints are rate-limited. The portal login self-heals legacy accounts by seeding a modern admin user transparently on first sign-in.

M02 · Access Management

Administrators create staff accounts (auto-numbered as USR001, USR002…), assign a role, and grant granular permissions through a picker that mirrors the sidebar. Admin/superadmin get full access; custom roles get exactly what is ticked. Users can be activated, deactivated, have passwords reset, and be deleted (with superadmin protections).

M03 · Client Management

The client master captures identity, staffing, currency/timezone and integration configuration. Clients can be onboarded, soft-dropped (with reason and reversible re-onboarding), hard-deleted (cascading across all related collections), and searched full-text. Billing fields are deliberately read-only here — they are owned by the ledger.

M04 · Client Portal Access

A provisioning console where administrators enable the portal per client, seed the first admin email/password, reveal a saved password when a client forgets it, and copy the client login URL. Summary tiles show total portals, active portals and pending setups.

M05 · Google Ads Integration

Connects a client via a validated 10-digit customer ID and synchronises campaigns, per-day campaign metrics (impressions, clicks, cost, conversions, impression share, click-type breakdown) and keyword-level performance. KPIs (CTR, CPC, CPA, conversion rate, ROAS) are enriched on each metric. Spend is reconciled into the billing ledger.

M06 · Meta Ads Integration

A one-click wizard attaches a Meta ad account and Facebook page. The platform syncs campaigns, ad sets, ads and daily insights at three levels, plus lead-generation form definitions. Leads arrive in real time via signature-verified webhooks (with polling and a retry queue). Page access tokens are encrypted at rest; unmapped forms surface to an admin queue for assignment.

M07 · Lead Management

Leads are captured from Meta forms, click-to-WhatsApp, and manual entry, with full source/UTM attribution and the raw payload retained. Leads carry status, assignment, category (HAIR/SKIN), location and duplicate flags. Processing is idempotent so nothing is duplicated or lost.

M08 · Telecalling Sheet & Automation

The heart of the daily operation: a 26-column Excel-style sheet identical to the team's real workbook. Telecallers edit inline with auto-save; the system validates every value against the sheet vocabulary, auto-appends call history, auto-suggests the next reminder, and flips leads to DARMANT after five unconnected attempts. Work queues (due today, fresh, etc.) prioritise effort, and the sheet imports/exports the real Excel file (with data cleaning on import).

M09 · Daily Lead Data / Daily Entry

Per-day, per-client capture and roll-up of Meta and Google lead counts and spend, with auto-calculated totals and CPL, plus an agency-wide daily aggregate. "By Client" and "Lead Check" views support daily/weekly/monthly toggles.

M10 · Billing & Payments

Administrators record client payments (credits) and the platform debits ad spend automatically. Every entry is immutable and idempotent, carrying a running balance. Tools include reconciliation against the ad platform, admin adjustments, deep historical sync, and a guarded destructive reset.

M11 · Balance Monitoring & Alerts

Live ad-account balances are cached for fast reads. A header alert bell flags Meta accounts running low; a Balance Watch board buckets clients into Critical / Warning / Healthy so ads never auto-pause unnoticed.

M12 · Agency Dashboard

The performance-marketing command centre: date-scoped, per-client Meta and Google spend, leads, CPL and balance, with smart alerts ("low balance", "spending but zero leads"), recent activity, and top-performer ranking. Clicking any client drills into its detail page.

M13 · Client Ad Details

A single-client deep dive with Google Ads, Meta Ads and EOD tabs — account info, billing, KPI grids, spend-trend charts, campaigns with expandable keywords, the full leads table, live resync, exports and an AI insights panel.

M14 · Ads Comparison

Side-by-side performance of every linked client across Google and Meta tabs, with KPI cards, charts, rankings (top performer, best value, needs review) and a shared date range and search.

M15 · Analytics & Reports

Per-client trend analysis, daily and monthly overviews, day-over-day change, a full billing ledger with balance timeline, and today/yesterday/week/month spend overviews — all in IST, all reading the shared metric definitions.

M16–M18 · Total Leads, EOD Report & Monthly Abstract

Total Leads is a month-tabbed pivot of leads by client and day. The EOD Report mirrors the team's Google Sheet exactly — day and month target-vs-achieved, appointment pipeline (yesterday → +5 days), and a day summary with source breakdown and conversion block; targets are editable per client. The Monthly Abstract is a per-day grid banded into source, status, calls and appointments/conversion columns, with editable override cells.

M19–M20 · Client Portal (Today & Users)

The portal's live "today" view shows six KPI tiles and six source cards, every number clickable through to a filtered leads view, auto-refreshing ~30s. Client admins manage their own telecaller and admin logins, with last-admin and self-deletion protections.

M21 · AI Campaign Insights

On demand, the platform sends a performance snapshot to Google Gemini and returns a plain-language analysis: overall summary, an A–F grade, strengths, improvements, five actionable strategies to raise leads / lower CPL, urgent red-flags and KPIs to watch — with graceful degradation if the AI service is unavailable.

M22–M25 · Vaults, Content Calendar & Observability

Encrypted client and personal credential vaults store platform logins (personal entries support sharing). A content calendar plans and tracks social posts per client (Reel/Static/Carousel) with an approval status. Sync observability exposes run history, the retry queue and the raw-lead audit for operational assurance.

29Acceptance Criteria

Representative acceptance criteria in Given–When–Then form. Each maps to a business requirement.

Maps toAcceptance Criterion

BR-CL-02Given a dropped client, when re-onboarded, then the client returns to active with full prior history intact.
