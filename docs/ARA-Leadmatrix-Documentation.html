Product Overview

What Leadmatrix is, and why it exists.

Leadmatrix is the internal software platform ARA Discoveries Tech built to run day-to-day operations for cosmetic and dermatology clinic clients. This section explains what it does, who uses it, and the problem it solves.

Executive summary

ARA Leadmatrix consolidates three previously separate tools into a single web application: Meta and Google's ad-account dashboards, a lead-tracking spreadsheet, and a Google Sheet for end-of-day telecaller reports. It gives the agency a live, per-client view of ad performance and lead outcomes, and gives clients a dedicated portal to see their own numbers in real time — replacing "please send today's numbers" emails with self-serve access.

The problem it solves

Before Leadmatrix, running a client's operations meant switching between three tools every day, reconciling numbers that never quite matched, and manually preparing reports for the client each evening. Low ad-account balances would go unnoticed until campaigns auto-paused, losing hours of leads. Clients had to wait for someone to screenshot data and email it. Leadmatrix removes that friction by putting every number the agency and the client care about behind one login.

Getting Started

Signing in and finding your way around.

Everything you need to log in for the first time and get oriented.

Prerequisites

  • A computer with a modern browser (Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge recommended)
  • Login credentials (email and password) issued by your admin
  • The application URL: leadmatrix.discovertechnologies.co

Signing in

  1. Open your browser and navigate to leadmatrix.discovertechnologies.co.

  2. Enter your registered email and password on the sign-in screen. Click the eye icon to reveal the password if you need to check.

  3. Click Sign In. You'll land on the Dashboard.

  4. To sign out, click your avatar in the top-right corner and choose Logout.

Note

If your login fails repeatedly, contact your admin for a password reset. Do not share your credentials — every user is tracked individually for audit purposes.

Module Reference

Every screen, explained.

Ten modules make up the agency-side experience, with a separate portal for clients. Each is documented below with what it shows, how to use it, and the key features to know about.

Dashboard

The default screen after sign-in. Answers "is anything on fire?" in about ten seconds.

Key elements

  • Greeting strip — Personalised greeting, date picker for reviewing any past day, refresh button
  • Quick Find search — Live-filters client cards as you type
  • Balance Watch panel — Every Meta ad account under ₹2,000, tiered as critical (below ₹1,000, red) or watch (below ₹2,000, amber), with a marker at the auto-pause threshold
  • Attention list — Clients spending money today but with zero leads returned
  • KPI cards — Total, Active, and Inactive client counts with platform-mix chips
  • Spotlight cards — Top Performer, Best Value, and Needs Review
  • Client-wise performance grid — One card per client with today's Meta and Google metrics side by side

Leads Check

A tabular view of lead volume and ad spend per client, broken down by day, week, or month.

  • Daily, Weekly, and Monthly presets, plus custom date-range picker
  • Columns show Google calls and website leads, Meta form and WhatsApp leads, totals, and Meta ad spend
  • Excel and PDF export via the buttons at the top-right

Clients

The client roster. Add, edit, and manage every client the agency works with.

  • Add Client — Fill in name, location, organisation type, and status
  • Link Meta ad account — Enter the ad account ID (prefixed with act_) via the Facebook icon
  • Link Google Ads — Enter the Customer ID (10-digit) via the Google icon
  • Portal user management — Grant clients access to their own portal via the Users tab
  • Permanent deletion — Removes the client and all associated records (leads, entries, ad-side data)
Watch out

Deleting a client cannot be undone. If retention is a possibility, mark the client as "dropped" instead — they'll disappear from the Dashboard but records remain.

Total Leads

A pivot view showing every client on rows and every day of the selected month on columns.

  • Month-tab navigation for quick historical comparison
  • Client search filter for narrowing to a single account
  • CSV export and print support

Ads Comparison

Multi-client ad performance comparison across Google and Meta.

  • Separate tabs for Google Ads and Meta Ads
  • Date range and search filters
  • Four spotlight cards: Top Spender, Top Lead Generator, Best CPL, Worst CPL
  • Full account table with Budget, Impressions, Clicks, CTR, CPC, Cost, Conversions, CPL
  • Best and worst CPL rows tinted in the table (green and red, respectively)

Client detail page

Accessed by clicking any client from the Dashboard, roster, or spotlight. Contains four tabs specific to that client.

Google Ads tab

Ad spend cards for today, yesterday, this week, this month. Performance summary. Campaign list with per-campaign metrics. Keyword list with quality scores. Daily performance chart.

Meta Ads tab

Live account balance (red warning when below ₹1,000), today's spend, lifetime spent. Full performance summary. Campaign / ad set / ad hierarchy. Per-lead table with edit-in-place.

EOD Report tab

Daily telecalling report with sources, call activity, connected counts, appointments, response breakdown, and a full leads-worked-today table.

Leads Table tab

15-column per-lead detail for the selected date range. Columns cover Date, Source, Name, Contact, Location, Category, First Call Date, Call Label, Response, Remarks, Next Follow-up, Status, Appointment Date, Follow-up count, and Latest history.

Client Portal

A separate application clients log in to. Isolated per client — each client sees only their own data.

  • Dedicated URL: leadmatrix.discovertechnologies.co/client-login
  • Portal Dashboard with EOD Report and Monthly Abstract tabs
  • Leads view with date range filters and Excel export
  • Data restricted to what telecallers actually entered on individual leads

Notifications

A persistent bell icon in the top bar, visible on every page.

  • Grey bell with no badge — no low-balance accounts
  • Amber bell with count — accounts in the watch band (₹1,000–₹1,999)
  • Red animated bell — accounts in the critical band (below ₹1,000)
  • Click to see the full list, grouped by severity; each row links to the client's detail page
  • Auto-refreshes every 90 seconds

Access Management

Role-based module access, editable per team member without code changes.

  • Toggle module access with checkboxes
  • Preset role bundles: SMM Starter, Admin, Telecaller
  • Superadmin override for troubleshooting
  • Users must sign out and back in for changes to take effect

Analytics

Longer-timeframe reports and trend analysis for management-level review.

Common Workflows

Standard recipes for the most-run tasks.

Short procedures for the situations you'll encounter routinely on the platform.

Topping up a low-balance Meta account

  1. Click the notification bell in the top bar (red badge indicates critical accounts).

  2. Click the client's row in the notification panel to open their detail page.

  3. Note the current balance shown on the Meta Ads tab.

  4. Open Meta Ads Manager in a separate tab and top up the ad account there — the actual funds transfer happens on Meta's platform, not in Leadmatrix.

  5. Return to Leadmatrix. Within ~90 seconds, the bell will reflect the new balance and the red badge should clear.

Investigating a client's dropped performance

  1. Open the client's detail page from the Dashboard or roster.

  2. On the Meta Ads tab, review the daily performance chart to identify the day performance changed.

  3. Check the Billing card — did the account balance run out on that day?

  4. Review the campaign list — are any campaigns paused or in an error state?

  5. Cross-reference the EOD Report tab — has telecaller activity also dropped, or only the ads?

Onboarding a new client

  1. Navigate to Clients and click + Add Client.

  2. Enter the client's name, place, organisation type, and status. Save.

  3. Link their Meta ad account via the Facebook icon (paste the act_ ID).

  4. Link their Google Ads customer ID via the Google icon.

  5. Set up portal access from the Users tab. Share the client login URL and credentials with them.

Providing client portal access

  1. Open the client's row in the Clients screen.

  2. Navigate to the Users tab.

  3. Add a new user with email, name, role (Admin or Telecaller), username, and password.

  4. Share leadmatrix.discovertechnologies.co/client-login and the credentials with the client.

Recent Enhancements

What has changed in the current release cycle.

Improvements shipped in the current development cycle, with the operational reasoning behind each.

Balance Watch panel

A visual fuel-gauge panel on the Dashboard shows every low-balance ad account with a marker at the ₹1,000 auto-pause threshold. Ops teams check this first each morning to identify accounts requiring immediate top-up.

Persistent notification bell

A bell icon in the top bar surfaces low-balance accounts from any screen. Auto-refreshes every 90 seconds, ensuring critical alerts are not missed even during deep-focus work on other modules.

Client portal cleanup

Client-facing EOD Report and Monthly Abstract now display only data captured directly from lead-level entries, hiding agency-side manual fields. Aligns the portal with client expectations and keeps agency internals private.

Client card name visibility

Client names on Dashboard cards now wrap to a second line rather than truncating with an ellipsis. Resolves ambiguity between similar client names (e.g. "…GloSkin Karaikal" vs "…GloSkin Karaikkudi").

Elevated quick-find search

The client search bar has been elevated to the top of the Dashboard, immediately below the greeting. Improves discoverability for new users who previously scrolled past the section-header search.

Leads Table administrative tab

A new administrative tab on the client detail page mirrors the client portal's leads view. Enables admins to see exactly what the client sees during support conversations.

Hard-delete with cascade

Client deletion is now permanent with a full cascade cleanup across daily entries, funds, leads, portal users, and ad-side records. Prevents orphan data from accumulating over the platform's lifetime.

Glossary

Terminology reference.

Every term you'll encounter in the platform, explained in plain language.

Ad Spend
The amount of money the agency has spent running ads for a client, in ₹.
Impressions
The number of times an ad appeared on someone's screen. One person seeing an ad multiple times counts each time.
Reach
The number of unique people who saw an ad. Different from impressions — reach counts each person once regardless of frequency.
Clicks
The number of times an ad was clicked or tapped.
CTR
Click-Through Rate. Clicks divided by impressions, expressed as a percentage. Higher indicates more engaging creative.
CPC
Cost per Click. Average amount paid for one click. Ad spend divided by clicks.
CPM
Cost per Mille. Cost per 1,000 impressions. Standard efficiency metric for broad-reach campaigns.
Conversion
A meaningful action taken by a lead after seeing an ad — a form fill, WhatsApp message, or call.
Lead
A person who has expressed genuine interest — typically via form submission, WhatsApp, or a call.
CPL
Cost per Lead. Ad spend divided by number of leads. Lower is better.
Meta Form Lead
A lead who filled a Facebook or Instagram Instant Form. Details arrive in real time.
WhatsApp Lead
A lead who initiated a WhatsApp conversation via a Meta ad's WhatsApp button.
Campaign
The top level of an ad account structure. A collection of ad sets with a common objective.
Ad Set
A sub-group within a campaign. Shares the campaign's objective but has its own audience and budget.
Quality Score
Google Ads' rating of keyword and ad relevance. Higher scores reduce click costs.
Auto-pause
Automatic suspension of campaigns when the ad account balance is exhausted. Campaigns resume after top-up.
EOD Report
End-of-Day Report. Daily summary of telecalling activity.
Monthly Abstract
A per-day grid view showing every EOD-tracked metric across a month.
Follow-up (FU)
A subsequent call made to a lead after the initial contact.
Response Label
The telecaller's outcome label for a lead: Hot, Warm, Cold, Consulted, Treatment Booked, Not Interested, etc.
Appointment Status
Where an appointment stands: Booked, Rescheduled, Completed, or Cancelled.
